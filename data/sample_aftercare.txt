Lip Filler Aftercare Instructions

Post-procedure care is essential to ensure optimal results and minimise the risk of complications following dermal filler augmentation of the lips.

Immediate Post-Treatment (0-24 hours)
Patients should avoid manipulation of the treated area for a minimum of 24 hours post-procedure. Application of cosmetics to the perioral region is contraindicated during this period. Consumption of alcohol should be avoided as it may exacerbate oedema and increase the risk of ecchymosis. Strenuous physical activity and exposure to extreme temperatures, including saunas and steam rooms, should be avoided.

Patients should apply a cold compress intermittently to the treated area to assist in reduction of post-procedural oedema. The compress should not be applied directly to the skin.

First Week
Patients should avoid dental procedures for a minimum of two weeks post-treatment. Sleeping in a supine position is recommended where possible to minimise gravitational displacement of the filler product. High-impact facial massage or manipulation should be avoided.

Warning Signs
Patients should seek immediate clinical review if they experience any of the following: blanching or mottling of the skin, severe unilateral pain disproportionate to the procedure, loss of sensation in the treated area, visual disturbances, or signs of systemic allergic reaction including urticaria, angioedema, or respiratory compromise.

Follow-up
A review appointment is recommended at two weeks post-procedure to assess the result and determine whether a top-up treatment is indicated.
