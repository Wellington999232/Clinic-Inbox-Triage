Vascular Occlusion Protocol

this is what we do when someone calls or messages about possible vo after filler treatment. its important everyone follows the same steps.

if a patient contacts the clinic saying they have white or pale skin after filler, pain that is getting worse, or skin that looks like a net pattern (mottling) they need to be seen straight away. dont tell them to wait and see. this is an emergency.

the receptionist should immediately get a clinician on the phone with the patient. dont try to give advice yourself if you are not a clinician. just get the clinician involved asap.

the clinician will decide if the patient needs to come in or go to hospital. if the clinician says come in, clear the schedule and see them within the hour. if they say hospital, call an ambulance or tell the patient to go straight to A&E.

hyaluronidase should always be available in the clinic for these cases. the clinic manager needs to check stock every month. if we run out this needs to be flagged immediately.

after the incident the clinician fills in an incident report form. this goes to the clinic manager. we review all VO incidents every quarter to see if our protocol needs updating.
